How to Properly Clean Your Monitor Without Damaging the Screen (Ultimate Guide)

Many users often notice a lot of dust or fingerprints on their screen after doing a screen dead pixel test, mistaking them for dead pixels.

But before you grab a paper towel or glass cleaner, STOP! Wrong cleaning methods can permanently damage your monitor's anti-glare coating, leading to a scratched or ruined screen.

This article will teach you how to safely and effectively clean your LCD, OLED, or MacBook screen like a professional.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Absolute Don'ts

Before we start, make sure you avoid these common mistakes:

  1. Don't use cleaners containing alcohol, ammonia, or acetone: Common glass cleaners like Windex will dissolve the special coatings on your screen surface.
  2. Don't use paper towels or toilet paper: Their fibers are rough and will leave tiny scratches (swirl marks) on your screen.
  3. Don't spray liquid directly on the screen: Liquid can flow into the bezel gaps and seep into the circuit board, causing a short circuit.

‚úÖ The Correct Cleaning Steps

Step 1: Power Off and Cool Down

Turn off your monitor, preferably unplug it. Let the screen cool down - it's easier to see dust and oil on a black screen.

Step 2: Remove Surface Dust (Dry Clean)

Before wiping, you must first remove large dust particles from the surface, otherwise the dust will scratch your screen like sandpaper during wiping.

  • Recommended tools: Use an air blower or soft brush.

Step 3: Use a Professional Wiping Cloth (The Secret Weapon)

This is the most critical step. You must use a high-quality microfiber cloth. This type of cloth absorbs oil instead of spreading it around.

Step 4: Stubborn Stain Treatment (Wet Clean)

If dry wiping can't remove fingerprints or oily marks, you need to use a small amount of specialized cleaning solution (or distilled water).

  1. Spray the cleaning solution on the cloth (remember not on the screen).
  2. Gently wipe the screen in circular motions.
  3. Wipe again with the dry side of the cloth to remove water marks.
